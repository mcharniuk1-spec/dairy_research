# KSE EA Theses Style Benchmark for Final V10

Source reviewed: Kyiv School of Economics, “MA Theses 2025,” Economic Analysis section, accessed May 11, 2026.

## Observed pattern

The Economic Analysis theses listed by KSE use direct research-question titles and policy/economic objects rather than technical model-first titles. Examples include war intensity and liquidity preferences, freight elasticity in wartime Ukraine, inflation attention thresholds, public debt and trade balance, employment opportunities for people with disabilities, DCFTA trade effects, and wartime land markets.

## Implication for this thesis

The final title and chapter flow should stay object-first: **Market Power in the Dairy Value Chain in Ukraine**. Econometric terminology belongs in the methodology and results chapters, not in the title or first introduction paragraph.

The writing should be compact and evaluative:

- state the economic problem first;
- define why the Ukrainian dairy chain is a credible case;
- define the two hypotheses early;
- make price transmission the method, not the research object;
- present data reliability before coefficients;
- interpret results through farms, processors, procurement channels, retailers, and consumers;
- keep policy implications connected to monitoring, payment terms, procurement data quality, and retail panels.

## Changes reflected in V10

- Introduction starts with the Ukrainian dairy-chain policy problem and delayed-payment/procurement context.
- Results are grouped by H1 and H2 rather than by model family.
- Main tables distinguish long-run slope evidence from adjustment evidence.
- Retail and ProZorro evidence is kept as mechanism evidence when the time window or product matching is weak.
- The conclusion is policy-relevant and avoids legal overclaiming.

## Limitations

This benchmark used the public KSE 2025 theses index and title-level style pattern, not full private grading notes. It is therefore a style and positioning benchmark, not a complete quality audit of every referenced thesis.
